Dataset details

International Migration Database

The International Migration database presents data showcasing flows and stocks of the total immigrant population and immigrant labour force together with data on acquisition of nationality.

I have the data from year 2000 to 2013.

Types and semantics of dataset:

  1. Country of birth/nationality - Contains the name of the country in which one is born or having nationality. (Categorical)
  2. Variable - Contains the factor by which migrations occur. (Categorical)
    1. Inflows of foreign population by nationality
    2. Outflows of foreign population by nationality
    3. Stock of foreign-born population by country of birth
    4. Inflows of asylum seekers by nationality
    5. Stock of foreign population by nationality
    6. Acquisition of nationality by country of former nationality
    7. Inflows of foreign workers by nationality
    8. Inflows of seasonal foreign workers by nationality
    9. Stock of foreign born labour by country of birth
    10. Stock of foreign labour by nationality
  3. Country - Contains the name of the country to migrate. (Categorical)
  4. year - Contains migration year. (Ordered)
  5. Value - Contains the number. (Quantitative)

Variable Details
Acquisition of nationality

Acquisition of citizenship refers to the mechanism by which certain foreign-born children acquire citizenship automatically by virtue of being born to parents who are U.S. citizens.

Foreign Population

The foreign population consists of people who still have the nationality of their home country. It may include people born in the host country. The difference across countries between the size of the foreign-born population and that of the foreign population depends on the rules governing the acquisition of citizenship in each country. This indicator is measured as a percentage of population.

Foreign-born population

The foreign-born population covers all people who have ever migrated from their country of birth to their current country of residence. The foreign-born population data shown here include people born abroad as nationals of their current country of residence. The difference across countries between the size of the foreign-born population and that of the foreign population depends on the rules governing the acquisition of citizenship in each country. This indicator is measured as a percentage of population.

Stocks of foreign-born population

This indicator presents the stock of foreign-born population, that is, the size of immigrant populations as measured in number of persons. Data on immigrant populations may be collected through registers, residence permits, statistical surveys, or censuses, depending on the country.

Effects of immigration

The effects of immigration on the total output and income of the U.S. economy can be studied by comparing output per worker and employment in states that have had large immigrant inflows with data from states that have few new foreign-born workers. This produces efficiency gains and boosts income per worker. At the same time, evidence is scant that immigrants diminish the employment opportunities of U.S.-born workers.
